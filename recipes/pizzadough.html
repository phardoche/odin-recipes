Preparation 2 hours | Cooking time 7-10 minutes | Quantity 8 pizzas (12 inches diameter)

Ingredients

Poolish

  • 300 ml water (tempered)
  • 300 gr flour (sieved)
  • 5 gr dry yeast
  • 5 gr honey

Dough

  • 1 poolish recipe (all)
  • 700 ml water (tempered)
  • 1250 gr flour
  • 40 gr salt

Steps

For the poolish

At least 48 hours prior to pizza cooking

  1. Add lukewarm water in a recipient with a lid.
  2. Dissolve the honey in the water.
  3. Add the yeast and stir to dissolve in the sugared water.
  4. Add the flour and stir briefly until homogeneous.
  5. Snap the lid and leave at room temperature (summer) or in the oven warmed for a short time (start oven only to feel the heat coming from burner).

For the dough

  1. In a large bowl (preferably with a lid), add the water and gently stir in the poolish with a spatula.
    2. Add the flour in 3 to 4 steps, stirring to mix it all with the liquid.
  2. When half the flour is incorporated, add the salt (adding the salt directly to the poolish may harm the yeast).
  3. When it is no longer possible to mix with the spatula, transfer the dough to a clean surface and knead with the hands, folding the dough to incorporate air for about 5 minutes.
  4. The dough will be quite sticky, cover with the bowl for 15 minutes and let it rest.
  5. Cover your hands with a little bit of olive oil, scrape the dough from the counter and fold it for 3 to 4 times by lifting by the middle with both hands, in order to create a balloon and stretch the surface until it is smooth and strong. From this moment, the top of the dough must remain on top for all steps until the end.
  6. Scrape any bits of flour or dried dough from the bowl and cover all its surface with olive oil.
  7. Drop the dough in the bowl and cover for 30 minutes.
  8. Cut the dough in equal parts and shape the dough balls by keeping the top on top, folding the outer part under it.
  9. Gently put the balls in a big plastic tray with a lid (a piece of parchemin paper for each ball will prevent them to stick together when they expand).
  10. Keep the tray in the fridge until the day of cooking. Take it out of the fridge at least for hour before shaping and cooking. I recommend to unclip the lid and to place a towel over the tray to absorb humidity. If the tray is shallow, cut a parchemin paper the size of the tray to put on top of the dough balls under the towel.

At that point, the dough balls can be frozen on a pan then kept in the freezer in a sealed bag or ziploc.
